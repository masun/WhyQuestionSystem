The word Minnesota comes from the Dakota name for the Minnesota River: Mnisota. The root mni (also spelled mini or minne) means "water". Mnisota can be translated as sky-tinted water or somewhat clouded water.Native Americans demonstrated the name to early settlers by dropping milk into water and calling it mnisota.Minnehaha Falls ("waterfall"), Minneiska ("white water"), Minneota ("much water"), Minnetonka ("big water"), Minnetrista ("crooked water"), and Minneapolis, a combination of mni and polis, the Greek word for "city".
